I'm not a foodie, but allow me to ramble for a bit.

---

I have a sweet tooth. The last time I saw the dentist he said I should avoid sweets. I did! For a day. Given the chance, I will go 
for a snack. A chocolate bar here, a cookie there, or a full dessert if it's available. That said, I obviously know they are not 
great for you in the long term. And they definitely don't count as a full meal -- trust me, multiple energy bars / Snickers don't 
work. But still, that's sometimes how I "cook" my weekend meals.

---

Did I mention I'm a lazy chef? My dinner is usually just some carbohydrates (rice / pasta), meat, vegetables thrown together. It's cooked, somewhat nutritious, but not great in flavour. This was fine when I lived alone.

Now that I live with my friends, I see they cook up much more elaborate dishes. Full on Christmas Turkeys, Moroccan chicken, apple crumble, and more. They take hours, and sometimes days just to prepare a dish. And they do this after work! When we have dinner together, each person provides their a dish. So I'm inspired / a bit peer-pressured into putting more effort to make sure it's not terrible.

---

Most of the time I don't know what to cook. Usually it's whatever I have left from last week, plus avoiding the same ingredients. Considering my usual way of cooking, it's rather easy, though I won't be cooking anything exciting. It's nice, but I'm not looking at improving my skills.

I envy others who put in the effort. Nay, I envy others who have _some_ cuisine in mind and cook something resembling it. My flatmates certainly fall in this bucket, with them knowing what to cook and not being afraid to try new things. Me? :shrugs:

---

One of my mutual friends is like me, i.e. spends a lot of time at work and doesn't want to think too hard about what to cook. Luckily, he has a responsive friend (Elizabeth, or "Eliza", he calls her) to give him ideas. She suggests recipes, instructions, and even what piece of fruit to pick at the shop. And the dishes taste great (he says)! He's eager to introduce me to her, says we'd be a good fit. I'm not sure? I'm not that desperate, and don't fully believe in match-making. Besides, she doesn't seem to be a chef herself, or even cook her own meals.

On the other hand, I _am_ tempted by prospect of tasting new dishes each day. I guess that's why Hello Fresh[*] and similar services exist. Baking kits do exist, and I used one to bake some Guinness bread -- fresh, nice, and doesn't taste like alcohol! But this proposal veers too close to microwaving a dish, which is weird since I don't mind meal deals. Maybe this is closer to microwaving a dish and calling it home-cooked.

---

Am I making sense?

[*]: Not sponsored. But if someone from there or a similar company is somehow interested...
